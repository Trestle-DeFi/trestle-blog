# Mitigating Sybil Attacks in Decentralized Applications

Sybil attacks pose a significant threat to decentralized applications, compromising the integrity of on-chain decision-making processes. These attacks involve creating multiple fake identities to manipulate the system, often by flooding it with malicious actors.

## The Engineering Challenge

Traditional approaches to addressing Sybil attacks rely on manual verification, which is not scalable, or utilize centralized solutions that compromise the decentralized nature of the application. Engineers often turn to standard tools like CAPTCHAs or manual reviews, but these methods are cumbersome and may not be effective against sophisticated attackers.

## A Decentralized Solution

Trestle offers a more elegant solution by leveraging decentralized identity and verification mechanisms. By utilizing a decentralized approach, Trestle enables applications to verify identities without relying on centralized authorities. This not only improves scalability but also enhances the security of the system.
