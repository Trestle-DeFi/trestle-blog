# Introduction
Decentralized Finance (DeFi) applications have grown exponentially in recent years, but with this growth comes new challenges. One of the significant concerns is the prevention of Sybil attacks, which can compromise the integrity of these applications.

# The Problem: Sybil Attacks in DeFi
Sybil attacks occur when an attacker creates multiple fake identities to manipulate the outcome of a decentralized application. This can lead to a range of issues, including vote manipulation, resource exploitation, and economic instability.

# The Current State of Sybil Resistance
Traditional solutions to Sybil attacks rely on centralized identity verification systems, which can be vulnerable to attacks and compromise user anonymity. Decentralized identity solutions, on the other hand, offer a more secure and private approach to identity verification.

# Engineering Approach: Decentralized Identity Verification
A decentralized identity verification system uses blockchain technology to store and manage user identities. This approach ensures that user identities are secure, private, and resistant to tampering. By leveraging zero-knowledge proofs and cryptographic techniques, decentralized identity verification systems can authenticate users without revealing sensitive information
