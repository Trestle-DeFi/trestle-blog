# Sybil Resistance in DeFi: A Technical Solution

## Problem Statement

Decentralized applications (dApps) in DeFi are vulnerable to Sybil attacks, where malicious actors create multiple fake identities to manipulate the system. This can lead to unfair outcomes, compromised security, and loss of trust in the ecosystem.

## Standard Approaches

Traditional solutions rely on manual verification processes, which are time-consuming and prone to errors. Some dApps employ automated tools, such as CAPTCHAs or IP-based blocking, but these are easily circumvented by sophisticated attackers.

## Engineering Approach

To address this challenge, we need a more sophisticated approach that combines cryptographic techniques with decentralized identity management. Our solution leverages a novel use case of zero-knowledge proofs (ZKPs) to verify user identities without revealing sensitive information.

The core idea is to use a ZKP-based protocol to prove possession of a unique identity without disclosing the actual identity. This allows users to demonstrate their identity without compromising their anonymity.
