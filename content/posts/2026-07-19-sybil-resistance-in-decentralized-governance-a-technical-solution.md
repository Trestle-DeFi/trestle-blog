# Sybil Resistance in Decentralized Governance: A Technical Solution

## Problem Statement

Decentralized governance is a crucial component of DeFi, enabling community-driven decision-making and voting processes. However, a significant challenge arises in the form of Sybil attacks, where malicious actors create multiple fake identities to manipulate voting outcomes. This problem undermines the integrity of decentralized governance and necessitates the development of robust identity verification and Sybil resistance mechanisms.

## Traditional Approaches

Existing solutions often rely on manual verification processes, which are time-consuming, labor-intensive, and prone to errors. Moreover, these methods do not provide a scalable or decentralized solution, as they typically involve centralized authorities or intermediaries. Some projects have attempted to address this issue using traditional cryptographic techniques, such as digital signatures or zero-knowledge proofs. However, these approaches often come with significant performance overheads and are not well-suited for large-scale decentralized applications.

Trestle offers a novel approach to Sybil resistance and identity verification, leveraging a decentralized identity management system that enables secure, scalable, and community-driven governance
